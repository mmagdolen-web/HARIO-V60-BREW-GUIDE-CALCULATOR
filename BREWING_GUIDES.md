# Complete Brewing Guides for Filter Coffee

Detailed step-by-step instructions for each brewing method. Use the calculator to determine your coffee and water amounts, then follow the method-specific guide below.

---

## 1. Hario V60

### Equipment Needed
- Hario V60 dripper (ceramic, glass, or plastic)
- V60-specific paper or cloth filters
- Gooseneck kettle (optional but recommended)
- Kitchen scale
- Timer
- Carafe or cup

### Recipe
- **Coffee:** 20-30g (depending on brew size)
- **Water:** 300-500ml at 95-98°C
- **Grind size:** Medium-fine (like granulated sugar)
- **Total time:** 3-4 minutes
- **Bloom time:** 45 seconds

### Step-by-Step Guide

**1. Prepare the equipment**
- Place filter in V60 dripper
- Pour hot water through filter to rinse (removes paper taste)
- Empty the carafe/cup
- Weigh your coffee and grind to medium-fine

**2. Add coffee**
- Place V60 on top of cup/carafe
- Pour ground coffee into filter
- Gently level the bed (tap sides if needed)
- Create a small depression in the center

**3. Bloom (0:00-0:45)**
- Start timer
- Pour water equal to 2× coffee weight (e.g., 40ml water for 20g coffee)
- Pour slowly in circular motions from center outward
- Ensure all grounds are saturated
- Wait 45 seconds without pouring

**4. First pour (0:45-1:30)**
- After bloom, begin pouring slowly in circles
- Maintain consistent water level (about 1cm above grounds)
- Pour until you reach 50% of total water weight
- Should take about 45 seconds

**5. Second pour (1:30-3:00)**
- When water level drops slightly, begin pouring again
- Continue in slow, circular motions
- Pour to 75% of total water by 2:30
- Pour final water by 3:00 mark
- Total brew time should be 3-4 minutes

**6. Finish**
- Remove V60 and let coffee drip out completely
- Discard used filter and grounds
- Swirl cup briefly and serve

### Key Tips
- **Consistency:** Grind size is critical. If brewing too slow, grind coarser. If too fast, grind finer.
- **Pour technique:** Use slow, controlled circular pours. Avoid aggressive pouring.
- **Temperature:** Water temp drops as it sits. Heat to 98°C and let cool slightly (95-98°C range is ideal).
- **Bloom importance:** Don't skip bloom. It releases CO₂ and promotes even extraction.

### Troubleshooting

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| Brew drains in <2:30 min | Grind too coarse | Use finer grind |
| Brew takes >4:30 min | Grind too fine | Use coarser grind |
| Tastes bitter | Over-extraction | Grind coarser or reduce brew time |
| Tastes sour | Under-extraction | Grind finer or increase brew time |
| Inconsistent results | Grind varies | Invest in burr grinder |

## 2. Chemex

### Equipment Needed
- Chemex brewer (3-cup, 6-cup, 8-cup, or 10-cup)
- Chemex bonded paper filters (thicker than V60)
- Gooseneck kettle
- Kitchen scale
- Timer
- Carafe (integrated in Chemex)

### Recipe
- **Coffee:** 25-40g (depending on size)
- **Water:** 400-600ml at 95-98°C
- **Grind size:** Medium-coarse (like sea salt)
- **Total time:** 4-4.5 minutes
- **Bloom time:** 45 seconds

### Step-by-Step Guide

**1. Prepare equipment**
- Unfold one Chemex filter and place in top of brewer
- Rinse with hot water to seal filter and preheat Chemex
- Empty any rinse water from carafe
- Grind coffee to medium-coarse

**2. Add coffee**
- Pour ground coffee into rinsed filter
- Level the bed gently
- Create slight depression in center

**3. Bloom (0:00-0:45)**
- Start timer
- Pour hot water equal to 2.5× coffee weight
- Pour from center outward in circles
- Saturate all grounds evenly
- Wait 45 seconds

**4. First pour (0:45-2:00)**
- Begin pouring in slow circles
- Pour water until reaching 50% of total water weight
- Should complete by 1:30-2:00 mark

**5. Second pour (2:00-3:30)**
- Pour in same technique to reach 75% of total water
- Complete by 3:00-3:30

**6. Final pour (3:30-4:00)**
- Pour remaining water slowly to reach total weight
- Finish pouring by 4:00 mark (total brew time 4-4:30 min)

**7. Finish**
- Remove top section and let final water drip through
- You'll hear water dripping—this indicates proper extraction
- When dripping slows, carefully pour into cup and serve

### Key Tips
- **Thick filters:** Chemex filters are 20% thicker than regular filters. Grind slightly coarser.
- **Clean cup:** The thick filters produce an exceptionally clean, bright cup.
- **Longer brew:** Chemex naturally brews slower due to filter thickness.
- **Patience:** Don't remove top section early. Let water drain completely.

### Troubleshooting

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| Brew too slow (>5 min) | Grind too fine | Use coarser grind |
| Brew too fast (<3:30) | Grind too coarse | Use finer grind |
| Over-extracted (bitter) | Grind too fine or too slow | Coarsen grind or pour faster |
| Under-extracted (sour) | Grind too coarse or water too cool | Finer grind or hotter water |
| Filter collapses | Water too hot | Let water cool to 95°C |

## 3. Aeropress

### Equipment Needed
- Aeropress brewer (with plunger and filter cap)
- Aeropress paper or metal filters
- Gooseneck kettle
- Kitchen scale
- Timer
- Cup or carafe

### Recipe
- **Coffee:** 15-20g
- **Water:** 200-300ml at 90-95°C (cooler than V60/Chemex)
- **Grind size:** Medium-fine
- **Total time:** 2-3 minutes
- **Bloom time:** 30 seconds

### Step-by-Step Guide

**1. Prepare equipment**
- Assemble Aeropress (plunger + chamber)
- Rinse with hot water to warm it
- Place filter in filter cap and rinse
- Screw cap onto Aeropress
- Place on top of cup
- Grind coffee to medium-fine

**2. Add coffee**
- Pour ground coffee into Aeropress chamber
- Level gently
- Start timer

**3. Bloom (0:00-0:30)**
- Pour hot water (cooler than V60 — aim for 90-95°C)
- Use water equal to 1.5× coffee weight
- Stir gently for 3-5 seconds with stirrer or spoon
- Wait 30 seconds

**4. Main pour (0:30-1:30)**
- Pour remaining water slowly to fill chamber nearly to top
- This should take about 1 minute
- Leave ~1cm at top

**5. Stir (1:30-1:45)**
- Stir gently in circular motions for 10-15 seconds
- This ensures even saturation before press

**6. Press (1:45-3:30)**
- Place plunger on top (don't push yet)
- Wait 10 seconds
- Press down slowly and steadily over 30-90 seconds
- You'll hear hissing—this is CO₂ escaping
- Press until you hear gurgling sound

**7. Finish**
- Remove from cup
- Plunge the seal to eject used grounds
- Serve immediately

### Key Tips
- **Temperature:** Aeropress uses cooler water (90-95°C) than other methods. This suits the immersion style.
- **Flexibility:** Press time changes flavor. Shorter press = lighter cup, longer = fuller body.
- **Clean cup:** Metal filters give more body; paper filters give cleaner cup.
- **Immediate serving:** Unlike other methods, serve Aeropress immediately for best flavor.

### Troubleshooting

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| Plunger hard to press | Grind too fine | Use coarser grind |
| Weak/thin cup | Under-extraction | Press slower or grind finer |
| Bitter taste | Over-extraction | Press faster or lower water temp |
| Sour taste | Under-extraction | Grind finer or wait longer before pressing |
| Inconsistent | Variables | Be consistent with grind and timing |

## 4. Kalita Wave

### Equipment Needed
- Kalita Wave dripper (Stainless Steel or ceramic)
- Kalita Wave-specific filters (white or brown)
- Gooseneck kettle
- Kitchen scale
- Timer
- Cup or carafe

### Recipe
- **Coffee:** 20-30g
- **Water:** 300-500ml at 95-98°C
- **Grind size:** Medium (slightly coarser than V60)
- **Total time:** 3.5-4 minutes
- **Bloom time:** 45 seconds

### Step-by-Step Guide

**1. Prepare equipment**
- Place Wave filter in Kalita dripper
- Rinse with hot water (removes paper taste)
- Empty cup/carafe
- Grind coffee to medium (consistency of sand)

**2. Add coffee**
- Pour ground coffee into filter
- Level the flat bed with gentle taps
- Create slight depression in center

**3. Bloom (0:00-0:45)**
- Start timer
- Pour water equal to 2× coffee weight
- Pour slowly in center, moving outward
- Ensure all grounds wet
- Wait 45 seconds

**4. First pour (0:45-1:45)**
- Pour slowly in circles
- Maintain even water level over flat bed
- Reach 50% of total water by 1:30-1:45

**5. Second pour (1:45-3:00)**
- Continue pouring in circles
- Reach 75% of total water by 2:45-3:00

**6. Final pour (3:00-3:45)**
- Pour remaining water
- Finish by 3:45-4:00 mark
- Total brew time: 3.5-4 minutes

**7. Finish**
- Let final water drain
- Remove dripper
- Serve and enjoy

### Key Tips
- **Flat bed advantage:** The flat-bottomed Kalita's wave filters ensure even saturation without channeling (uneven flow).
- **Consistent extraction:** The flat bed means less technique skill required compared to V60.
- **Medium grind:** Kalita works great with slightly coarser grind than V60.
- **Steady pours:** The flat bed is forgiving, so maintain steady, gentle pours.

### Troubleshooting

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| Brew too slow | Grind too fine | Use coarser grind |
| Brew too fast | Grind too coarse | Use finer grind |
| Over-extracted | Grind too fine | Coarsen or increase pour speed |
| Under-extracted | Grind too coarse | Fine grind or decrease pour speed |
| Uneven wetting | Pour technique | Start from center and move outward |

### Equipment Needed
- Any flat-bottomed dripper (Melitta, Bonavita, etc.)
- Appropriate filters

- Timer
- Cup or carafe

### Recipe
- **Coffee:** 20-30g
- **Water:** 300-500ml at 95-98°C

- **Bloom time:** 45 seconds

### Step-by-Step Guide

**1. Prepare equipment**
- Place filter in dripper
- Rinse with hot water
- Preheat cup/carafe with hot water
- Empty preheating water
- Grind coffee to medium

- Pour coffee into filter
- Level gently
- Pour water at 2× coffee weight

## Pro Tips for All Methods

### 1. Get a Scale
- Most important tool for consistency
- Measure both coffee and water by weight (grams/ml)
- Digital scales cost $15-30

### 2. Grind Fresh
- Grind immediately before brewing
- Pre-ground coffee loses volatile aromatics quickly
- Burr grinders ($30+) better than blade grinders

### 3. Use Good Water
- Filtered water preferred
- Mineral-rich or chlorinated water affects flavor
- Water temperature: 95-98°C (unless brewing Aeropress)

### 4. Keep Your Equipment Clean
- Rinse dripper after each brew
- Soak cloth filters in water (not paper)
- Use filtered water for rinsing

### 5. Practice Consistency
- Same coffee amount
- Same water amount
- Same grind size
- Same brewing technique
- Record what you do and the result

### 6. Taste and Adjust
- If coffee tastes sour → grind finer or brew longer
- If coffee tastes bitter → grind coarser or brew shorter
- Small adjustments have big effects

## Common Brewing Problems & Solutions

### Coffee Tastes Sour (Under-Extracted)
**Possible causes:**
- Grind too coarse
- Water not hot enough
- Brew time too short
- Bloom time too short

**Solutions:**
- Use finer grind (but not too fine)
- Heat water to 96-98°C
- Increase brew time by pouring slower
- Increase bloom time by 10-15 seconds

### Coffee Tastes Bitter (Over-Extracted)
**Possible causes:**
- Grind too fine
- Brew time too long
- Water too hot
- Too much stirring/agitation

**Solutions:**
- Use coarser grind
- Reduce brew time by pouring faster
- Cool water to 94-95°C
- Gentle, minimal stirring

### Brew Drains Too Fast (<2:30)
**Cause:** Grind too coarse

**Solution:** Go one notch finer on your grinder

### Brew Drains Too Slow (>4:30)
**Cause:** Grind too fine

**Solution:** Go one notch coarser on your grinder

### Inconsistent Results
**Causes:**
- Grind size varies (need better grinder)
- Water temperature varies
- Inconsistent pouring
- Using different brewing ratios

**Solution:**
- Use consistent equipment and technique
- Use scale for measurements
- Keep notes on what you change

## Water Temperature Guide

Different brewing methods prefer different water temperatures:

| Method | Temp | Notes |
|--------|------|-------|
| V60 | 95-98°C | Slightly hotter = brighter, more extraction |
| Chemex | 95-98°C | Use hotter end (98°C) for consistency |
| Aeropress | 90-95°C | Cooler than others; immersion style |
| Kalita | 95-98°C | Works well at standard temp |
| Pour Over | 95-98°C | Adjust within range based on preference |

**How to measure:** 
- Boil water (100°C)
- Wait 30 seconds → ~95°C
- Wait 60 seconds → ~93°C
- Use gooseneck kettle with thermometer for precision

## Grind Size Comparison

### Coarse (like sea salt)
- Used for: French press, cold brew, Chemex
- Brew time: Longer (4+ minutes)
- Extraction: Less

### Medium (like sand)
- Used for: Kalita, generic pour overs
- Brew time: 3-4 minutes
- Extraction: Balanced

### Medium-Fine (like granulated sugar)
- Used for: V60, Aeropress
- Brew time: 2-3 minutes
- Extraction: More

### Fine (like powder)
- Used for: Espresso
- **Don't use for pour over!**
- Clogs filters, over-extracts
